To revoke Grok's access to your X account (or more precisely, to manage and limit what Grok/xAI can do with your account data after you authorized login/access), you have a couple of main options depending on what exactly bothers you. Grok is built by xAI and integrated directly with X (formerly Twitter), so it's not always treated as a traditional "third-party app." However, you can control permissions, data sharing, and personalization.

### 1. Revoke App/Session Access (If Grok Appears as a Connected App)
Grok login often uses X's OAuth system, so it may show up in your connected apps/sessions.

- Log in to X (on web/desktop or app) at x.com.
- Go to **Settings and privacy** (usually under "More" in the left sidebar or your profile menu).
- Select **Security and account access** > **Apps and sessions** (or directly visit https://x.com/settings/applications or https://twitter.com/settings/applications).
- You'll see a list of connected apps and active sessions.
- Look for anything related to "Grok," "xAI," or "Grok AI" (it might be listed under xAI or similar).
- Click on it to view details, then select **Revoke access** (or "Revoke" button next to it).

This will disconnect that specific authorization. Note: If Grok doesn't appear here (common for native integrations), proceed to the next steps for data controls.

### 2. Disable Grok Data Sharing, Personalization, and Training (Most Common Fix)
When you log in to Grok via X, it often enables data sharing by default (e.g., using your posts/interactions for training Grok or personalizing responses). You can turn this off:

- Log in to X on a **web browser/desktop** (some settings are limited or unavailable on mobile apps).
- Click **More** (three dots/lines) in the left menu > **Settings and privacy**.
- Go to **Privacy and safety**.
- Scroll to and select **Grok** (or **Grok & Third-party Collaborators** / **Data sharing and personalization** > **Grok**).
- You'll see options like:
  - "Allow your posts as well as your interactions, inputs, and results with Grok to be used for training and fine-tuning"
  - "Allow X to personalize your experience with Grok"
  - "Allow Grok to remember your conversation history"
- **Uncheck/disable** the ones you don't want (especially the training/data use box).
- If you've chatted with Grok before, look for a **Delete conversation history** button and confirm deletion.

This prevents Grok from using your data moving forward and limits personalization. Some users report this is under https://x.com/settings/grok_settings directly.

### Additional Tips
- If you want stronger privacy, make your X account private (**Settings and privacy** > **Privacy and safety** > **Audience and tagging** > Enable "Protect your posts"). Private accounts often restrict data use for AI training.
- Logging out of Grok sessions or clearing browser data/cookies can help reset some access, but the above settings are the proper way.
- If nothing shows up or it doesn't resolve your concern, check X's help center directly at https://help.x.com/en/managing-your-account/connect-or-revoke-access-to-third-party-apps for the latest official steps (they update periodically).
- As a last resort, you could log out everywhere (**Settings and privacy** > **Security and account access** > **Apps and sessions** > Log out of all sessions) and re-authorize only what you need, but that affects all logins.

These steps should stop unwanted access or data usage without deleting your X account. If the interface looks different (X changes things often), search within your X settings for "Grok" or "apps." Let me know if you run into issues!
